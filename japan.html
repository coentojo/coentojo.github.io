GINZA
On the weekends in Ginza the streets are completely closed to cars, which makes walking around much more enjoyable. The weather was perfect and the shopping was even better. A 7 story Uniqlo and Dover St. Market made for fun afternoon.
TOKYO FASHION
I had a great time in Tokyo just looking at the way people dress. They are bold and confident in what they wear. Even the kids get into it.
DAIKANYAMA
Store: Journey
Daikanyama is quiet and less crowded compared to other districts in Tokyo. There are tons of shops and small cafes where you can sit outside and enjoy the weather. I WANT TO LIVE HERE.
YOKOHAMA
(CHINATOWN)
FIRST STOP: SIOMAY
The Chinatown is completely packed with people crowding the food carts and food stalls. Had to try the siomay, one of my favorite Dim Sum dishes. Unfortunately, it was just alright.
A SUNDAY AFTERNOON
These guys are enjoying all the delicious baos, siomays, and other finger foods Chinatown has to offer.
PANDA MAN
This guy sells dessert baos in the shape of pandas...PANDAS.
PREPARATION
These ladies are churning out delicious xiao long baos, (xlb's as some weirdos call it) by hand. Some say the juiciness comes from the meat, some say the juice is injected afterwards...
RESULT
4 different xiao long baos. Vegetarian, seafood, chicken, and pork. The classic pork was ze best. They were nice and crispy on the bottom but chewy on the outside and of course soupy-goodness on the inside.
SAKURA
(UENO PARK)
EARLY
Took the train to Ueno park to check see if Sakura was in full bloom. Unfortunately, it was a bit too early in the season. This area must be absolutely beautiful when the cherry blossoms are in full bloom.
These homeless guys were quite friendly or quite creepy. Either way, they helped us take pictures under the blooming cherry blossoms.
Not many trees were in full bloom, but we were lucky enough to catch a few. These trees are just beautiful, especially during a nice, sunny spring afternoon.
